Do video games cause violence?

''A quick look at whether games are to blame!''

Introduction and motivation
During the past several decades, video games have been at the centre of blame for gun violence in America.
News broadcasts often state they are the cause of school shootings and murder.
Many people either agree or differ with this statement with their opinion based on arguments like ''they're just games''
and ''They're too violent''.
These ''arguments'' are not based on scientific evidence however, and are the result of a biased perspective. 
This research aims to look at scientific facts rather than biased opinions.
While doing research, I hope to find out the truth, so that I can assuredly answer the question:
''do video games cause violence?''

 

What does gaming do to your well-being?

Needless to say, gaming can be an extremely stressful way to spend your time.

While playing, the gamer has to keep track of multiple stimuli all at once and,
when they fail to do so, they often die in the game or have to start over.
While asking people, I discovered many of them argue

that this is what makes gaming unhealthy.
The need to multitask and the punishment for failing to do so

may be what many people consider unhealthy, but is that true?

One study done by the ''behavioural and brain science journal'' in 2015 found that playing video games

increases the cognitive abilities drastically.

In the study, participants were asked to play ''Mario 64'' every day for about thirty minutes.

After two months they took a test and it turned out their grades greatly increased.

The study stated that the effect gaming has on your brain is even more beneficial than playing so-called

''brain-games'' which are specifically designed to increase attention, spatial thinking among other areas of cognitive function. 

 

Another study on the subject proved that when participants played games,

their attention levels increased rather than decrease. 

 

All of this came as a surprise for me but it turns out that gaming can actually make you more intelligent.

Everything, however, should be done in moderation. Addicted gamers might get smarter while gaming

but they will surely lack the benefits of playing sports and being outdoors.

Also, sitting still indoors for multiple hours a day can have many drawbacks like decreased vital functions,

muscle loss and a lack of social abilities. 

 

All in all, gaming holds the potential to be one of the most beneficial ways to spend your leisure time.

However, it goes without saying that you should be mindful that you get frequent exercise

and have enough social connections. But that regards to virtually every activity.

 

Does the growing number of gamers correspond to the amount of crime?

More and more games are being published every day; and, due to media, it seems like

the amount of crime and deaths due to violence is increasing too.

During his speech, even Trump accused the game industry of gun violence in America. 

 

I was curious whether these statements are facts or fiction.

I gathered data and found that while the amount of violent games is increasing,

the number of deaths due to homicide is not. You can see this in the graph below.

This contradicts to the idea that video games are causing

American society to become more brutal. In fact, it is quite the opposite.

 

It is important to consider that this decrease in violence is not necessarily caused by games

because many other factors come into play like economics, racism and starvation.

It is, however, good to see that games did not cause an increase in the amount of violence in America.

We can conclude that there is little to no relationship between the number of people

that nationally game and the number of deaths due to violence.

It is, however, important to realise that the number of school shootings is going up.

My hypothesis for this is that for about 99.98% of society,

games pose no serious risk, while the remainder gets prone to becoming a killer. 

 

To support this idea, I looked up interviews of criminals and found out that from the sixteen interviewed criminals,

four said games played an important role in their lives.

This might seem like a lot, but you should keep in mind that nearly 70% of Americans play video games

(these include mobile games too.)

This means that people that play games have actually a lower risk of becoming a criminal!

Logical reasoning would suggest that the more youngsters game,

the more they stay inside and thus the less likely it is for them to get into fights. 

 

Does gaming cause you to be more violent?

The idea that gaming results in violence is largely based on the thought that it makes us insensitive to it.

People started getting concerned when the gaming industry started to become more popular

during the end of the 20th giving scientists the idea research the topic.

They did this by having young children play video games before asking them to feed someone spicey food.

The participants that played games were more likely to feed spicier food and thus the researches concluded

they were more aggressive. 

I reckon that the tests differ too much from reality making them unviable with a very weak connection. 

 

It now seems that all research done on the subject was useless, this, however, is not the case.

In one groundbreaking study, participants were asked to play a video game.

After doing this, they took a brain scan.

Interestingly, the areas in the brain used when committing crimes still looked the same

while other parts positively changed. This means that on a neurologic level gaming does not have any negative effects.

 

Conclusion

While gaming is a very intense activity, it does not posse any negative risk regarding growing violent.

It holds the ability to be good for you as long as it is done in moderation.

To sum things up, gaming does not generate violence and can actually reduce it by keeping teenagers off the streets.
