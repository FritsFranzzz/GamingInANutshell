Health Effects Of Gaming

Gaming is one of the most intense activities there is.

Focusing and being exposed to stress for a period must certainly have tremendous impacts.

On this page, we will take a closer look at just that! 

 

Benefits of gaming

  • ''Relaxing and having fun''

Gaming can be a great way to sit back and relax,

thereby holding the ability to provide an escape of reality as well.

A recent report even shows that over 40% of people game.

 

  • ''Cognitive functions''

Playing video games has profound benefits for your brain.

Studies show that playing at least half an hour a day increases your focus and hand-eye coordination.

These benefits can come to use at school resulting in gamers having better grades than non-gamers.

Playing games also contribute to brain functions like creativity, perseverance and many more.

 

  • ''Emotional progression''

Losing can help you to deal with failure. For this reason,

we believe gaming and thus failing can help players deal more positively

with real-life situations. Achieving your goals in games can also increase your self-esteem,

increase determination and make you more positive. 

 

  • ''Social intelligence''

When playing games, people often find themselves having to socialize.

The stereotype gamers are introverts with little friends making gaming

a great way to help them increase their social capabilities,

give them new abilities like increased communication skills

and most importantly give them new friends.

 

Risks of gaming

  • ''Incorrect balance''

As seen above, gaming has many benefits if done in proportion.

Playing all day long prevents you from meeting new people and

spending time doing school homework.

Games can also be quite expensive and contain certain types of gambling opponents

which children should not get exposed to at a young age. Gaming for extended periods is therefore not recommended.

 

  • ''Game addiction''

Not being able to stop playing can have huge impacts on your daily life.

I have suffered from this type of addiction.

The worst part was the stress that it generates and the lack of human contact it brings with it.

 

  • ''Physical problems''

Excessive use of video games could lead to insufficient amounts of exercise,

which is the cause of all sorts of problems.

 Looking at a screen for multiple hours a day could also alter your sleep rhythm

by preventing your brain from making melatonin which is

one of the substances responsible for making you feel sleepy.

 

Conclusion

Gaming holds the potential to be a great way to increase your overall happiness,

however, this can only be so when done in a balanced and non-excessive way.
